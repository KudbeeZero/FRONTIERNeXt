**Key Insights**
- The debit→effect seam is the systemic weakness: every paid action (`unlock`, `upgrade`, `deploy`, `fire`) spends first and applies effects second with no transaction/lock spanning both; H1/H2/H6 are all instances. One per-player serialization primitive fixes the class.
- The engagement state machine itself (in_flight→intercepted/impacted, magazine decrement-on-attempt, earliest-feasible-first layering) is clean and synchronous — single-threaded resolution at launch avoids fire-while-resolving races by construction. The bugs are at its edges: persistence (H5), credit lookup after deletion (H4), and the absent impact phase (H3).
- Math layer is well-guarded (slerp degenerate fallback, acos clamps, exhaustiveness guard in altitudeAt, catalog invariant test); residual risks are the dateline lerp (H8) and unvalidated intercept-envelope fields (H10).
- `docs/handoff/agent-memory.md` was absent at shift start; also the checkout flipped from pr-9 content to `claude/overnight-handoff-protocol-9csemq` mid-review — agents sharing one working tree should pin reads to a ref (`git show origin/pr-9:path`) as done here.
